Lyle Myler explains the origins of the Bureau of Reclamation.

Driven by growing public pressure from westerners, in the late 1800s, Congress passed a series of acts to encourage and study the development of irrigation in the west. But it wasn’t until President Theodore Roosevelt signed the Reclamation Act in 1902 that the federal government seriously invested in western irrigation projects. Then the newly-minted U.S. Reclamation ServiceU.S. Reclamation Service began to build dams and reservoirs to store water and essentially “re-time” high spring flows to deliver a steady water supply to agricultural users and municipalities throughout the year. They authorized 25 projects between 1903 and 1906.

Pathfinder Dam is situated just below the confluence of the Sweetwater and the North Platte rivers. The granite face reaches 214 feet high from the bottom of Fremont Canyon.

Built from 1905-1909, Pathfinder Dam was a tremendous undertaking, particularly for the time period. Four people died during its construction.
